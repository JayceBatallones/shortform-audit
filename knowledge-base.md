## 1. Ideal Viewer Clarity

The single highest-leverage factor. The reel that nailed this (Mino Lee's "ideal viewer") pulled 54K likes and 33K comments.

### What to look for
- **Can you name one specific person this script is for?** Not a demographic. A real person — a friend you talk to daily, or a past version of yourself.
- **Does it sound like talking to THEM, or broadcasting to everyone?** If it reads like a blog post, it needs a rewrite as a conversation with that one person.
- **Is it solving THEIR specific problem, or a generic one?** "How to grow on social media" is generic. "Why you've been posting for 6 months and still have 200 followers" is specific.

### Red flags
- Script uses "everyone" or "people" instead of "you"
- Problem described is too broad to be actionable
- Could apply to any niche — not targeted enough

> "Eight billion people on this planet — no. You know the exact 100,000 person tiny subset of the internet that will resonate so deeply with your message. And the rest of the people can fuck off." — Mino Lee (54K likes)

## 2. Idea Strength

The idea matters more than the hook, more than the storytelling, more than the editing. A weak idea with a great hook still dies.

### What to look for
- **Can you explain the video's value in one sentence?** If you can't, the idea isn't clear enough.
- **Would the ideal viewer screenshot this or send it to a friend?** If not, the idea isn't strong enough.
- **Has this concept been validated?** 70% of content should be based on ideas the market has already proven work (outliers in the niche). Only 10% should be untested experiments.
- **Is the idea a "don't" or a "do"?** "Don't" framing outperforms "do" framing. Self-protection instinct > self-improvement motivation. "Stop doing X" > "Start doing Y."

### The 70/20/10 content mix (credited to Caleb, via @personalbrandlaunch)
- **70%** — Proven concepts: outliers from your niche that the market has validated
- **20%** — Iterations: templatize YOUR best-performing content, recreate with small adjustments
- **10%** — Experiments: things you've always wanted to try (this is where breakout virality comes from)

## 3. Hook Patterns

If the hook doesn't land, nothing else matters. First 3 seconds decide everything.

### Hook length rule
**Keep hooks between 3-8 words.** The hook is a door, not a hallway. Get in, create the open loop, move on.

### Proven hook patterns (ranked by observed engagement)

| Pattern | Example | Why it works | Source engagement |
|---------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Singular promise | "Here is the ONE thing you need to know" | Reduces cognitive load, promises clarity | 54K likes |
| Question + pain point | "What separates those who blow up from those who get nothing?" | Viewer self-selects, needs the answer | 1.2K likes |
| Controversial claim | "Anybody can become a famous influencer" | Creates tension, forces engagement | 1.8K likes |
| Negation chain | "It's NOT your niche, NOT your captions, NOT who you copy" | Eliminates wrong beliefs, builds curiosity | 54K likes |
| Authority + shock | "Alex Hormozi says study the porn industry" | Borrows credibility + unexpected juxtaposition | 9.3K likes |
| Credibility stack | "I've helped many students go viral, here are the takeaways" | Establishes trust immediately | 16 likes (new post) |
| Smart vs. dumb framing | "The dumb creators do nothing, but the smart creators..." | Nobody wants to be in the dumb category | 2.3K likes |

### Hook templates (plug-and-play)
- "So I heard that if you..." — curiosity + hearsay framing
- "I will never understand..." — strong opinion, invites agreement/debate
- "I tried [crazy thing] for [time]" — experiment format, built-in narrative
- "Is it possible to [X]?" — open question, viewer needs the answer
- "In the next 60 seconds, I'm going to completely rewire how you think about [X]" — bold time-bound promise
- "I hate [X]" — polarizing, forces a reaction
- "If you're a [specific identity]..." — viewer self-selects instantly

### Hook red flags
- Starts with "Hey guys" or "So today I want to talk about" — delete and start with the value
- Takes more than 5 seconds to get to the promise — tighten it
- Hook is longer than 8 words — trim it down
- Doesn't create an open loop or unanswered question — viewer needs a reason to stay
- Tells the viewer what to do before earning trust — they don't know you yet

## 4. Script Structure

Every short-form script follows one master framework: **HOOK + STORY [SETUP + JOURNEY] + CALL-TO-ACTION**

The goal of every script is to **shift a belief.** If the viewer thinks the same thing before and after watching, the video failed.

### Word count targets
Use these as guardrails when scripting — going over means you're padding, going under means you might be too thin:

| Duration | Word count | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| 60s | 120-180 words | Tight, single-idea delivery |
| 90s | 220-230 words | Room for one story + framework |
| 120s | 300-360 words | Full narrative arc possible |

### Story ideas (pick one per video)
Every video needs a story engine. Choose one:

1. **Change** — something shifted in your world
2. **Transformation** — before/after with proof
3. **Lesson** — what you learned the hard way
4. **Win** — a specific result with numbers
5. **Loss** — what went wrong and why it matters
6. **My story** — personal origin, identity, journey
7. **Big goal** — where you're headed and why

### Story structure
Follow the classic arc — don't skip steps:

**Exposition → Rising action → Climax → Resolution**

Within this arc, the script breaks into two halves:

**SETUP** (the "why should I care" half):
- Who you are + why it matters to the viewer
- Current situation — ground the viewer in reality
- Clear stakes/consequences — what happens if they don't act
- Specific numbers/timeframe — vague = forgettable, specific = credible
- Pattern interrupt moment — the thing that breaks expectations

**JOURNEY** (the "what happened" half):
- Main challenge/problem faced
- Key obstacles — what made it hard
- Specific numbers ($, time, dates) — anchors the story in reality
- Real locations/people — makes it tangible
- Unexpected twists — the thing the viewer didn't see coming
- Building tension — each beat should raise the stakes

### Storytelling formats (pick one)
The format shapes how the story is delivered visually:

1. **Voiceover** — narration over footage, more produced feel
2. **Shot/angle change** — direct-to-camera with visual variety to maintain attention
3. **Audio + B-roll + Text** — layered format, highest production but highest retention

### Funnel stage clarity
Every script should serve ONE funnel stage:

| Stage | Content Type | Goal | Viewer feels |
|-------|-------------|------|-------------|
| Top of funnel | Light educational, tips | Views & reach | "Huh, that's interesting" |
| Middle of funnel | Storytelling, personal, vulnerable | Followers & connection | "I like this person" |
| Bottom of funnel | Transformations, proof, results | Leads & conversions | "I want what they have" |

If a script tries to serve all three, it serves none. Flag this.

### Pacing rules
- **Density over length.** A 44-second reel with 5 actionable points outperforms a 2-minute reel with 1 point padded out.
- **No dead air between ideas.** Every sentence should either deliver value or create anticipation for the next point.
- **List formats work.** Numbered points, "X things," frameworks with names (like "the 70/20/10 rule") — these give the viewer a mental scaffold.
- **Ultra-short can dominate.** A 12-second text-overlay reel got 25K likes and 6K comments. Don't add length for length's sake.

## 5. Authenticity & Vulnerability

Strengths make people like you. Weaknesses make people love you. This is the difference between a following and a fandom.

### What to look for
- **Does the script show any real weakness, failure, or uncomfortable truth?** Not performative vulnerability — actual flaws.
- **Does it sound like the creator, or like "a content creator"?** Authentic style often comes from childhood media consumption, not industry trends.
- **Is it sharing the lows, not just the wins?** Everyone shows what they're good at. Showing darker sides authentically is the differentiator.
- **Self-deprecating humor?** Disarms the audience, makes heavy topics approachable, inherently shareable.

### Examples from high-performing reels
- "I used to be a man-child in relationships" — 969 likes on a 7-second reel
- "As a recovered goon, I thought what the fuck?" — 9.3K likes
- "If you grew up being an insecure, socially anxious kid like me" — 1.8K likes

### Red flags
- Script is all wins, all expertise, no humanity
- Vulnerability feels calculated or performative
- Creator voice sounds like every other creator in the niche

## 6. Value Delivery

### What to look for
- **Giving or extracting?** If the creator is thinking about likes, follows, and comments while scripting — they're in extraction mode. Value-first removes self-consciousness and naturally creates better content.
- **Actionable RIGHT NOW?** Frameworks, checklists, numbered steps > vague inspiration. "Break your viral video into these 7 components" > "Just be consistent."
- **Passes the "so what" test?** After every point, imagine the viewer saying "so what?" If you can't answer, the point isn't valuable enough.

### The 7-component breakdown (for templatizing viral content, via @personalbrandlaunch)
1. The hook (verbal or visual)
2. The structure
3. The format
4. The editing approach
5. The topic selection
6. The value proposition
7. The CTA

## 7. CTA Strategy

### CTA types matched to goals

| Goal | CTA Type | Example | When to use |
|------|----------|---------|------------|
| Lead generation | Comment keyword + free guide | "Comment 'viewer' for the guide" | When you have a lead magnet that solves the video's problem |
| Client acquisition | Comment keyword + service pitch | "Comment 'SMM' for more info" | When your content reveals how hard the work is (offer to do it for them) |
| Audience growth | Follow CTA | "Follow for more" | When the video teases upcoming content |
| Trust building | No CTA / pure value | Just end with the insight | When you want depth of connection over metrics |
| Community building | Inspirational / conversational | "Drop what you've been doing with AI" | When you want discussion, not leads |
| Reaction bait | Emoji/identity prompt | "Drop a [emoji] if..." | When you want fast, low-friction engagement |
| Shared experience | Relatability prompt | "Comment if you've ever..." | When the video hits a universal feeling |

### CTA rules
- **Plant it mid-video, close it at the end.** Double mention massively outperforms single end-card CTA. (Mino Lee's double CTA → 33K comments)
- **CTA should feel like a natural extension of the value, not a pivot.**
- **Comment-keyword CTAs are algorithm multipliers.** Every comment is an engagement signal.
- **Pain relief > education in CTAs.** "We'll do it for you" converts harder than "here's how to do it" when the content just showed how much work it is.

## 8. Pre-Film Checklist

- [ ] **Ideal viewer:** Can you name the one person you're talking to?
- [ ] **Idea strength:** Would that person screenshot or share this?
- [ ] **Hook:** Does the first sentence create an open loop or make a bold promise?
- [ ] **Trust before instruction:** Are you earning credibility before telling them what to do?
- [ ] **"Don't" > "Do":** Can any "do this" advice be reframed as "stop doing this"?
- [ ] **Story engine:** Does the script have a clear story type (change, transformation, lesson, win, loss, my story, big goal)?
- [ ] **Setup + Journey:** Does the story have clear stakes, specific numbers, and building tension?
- [ ] **Word count:** Is the script within the target range for its duration?
- [ ] **Funnel clarity:** Is this clearly top/middle/bottom of funnel — not all three?
- [ ] **Vulnerability:** Does this show something real, not just a highlight reel?
- [ ] **Density:** Can you cut 20% of the words without losing value?
- [ ] **CTA match:** Does the CTA match the actual goal for this video?
- [ ] **The "so what" test:** After each point, can you answer "so what does this mean for you"?

## 9. Engagement Correlations (Data-Backed)

| Factor | Low engagement | High engagement |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Length | Padded, one idea stretched | Dense value OR ultra-short (<15s) |
| CTA type | "Follow for more" | Comment keyword with deliverable |
| Framing | "Here's what to do" | "Here's what NOT to do" / "Smart vs dumb" |
| Tone | Polished, scripted feel | Raw, conversational, profanity OK |
| Vulnerability | All wins, all expertise | Admits flaws, past mistakes, growth |
| Hook type | Gradual setup | Immediate bold claim or question |
| Cross-niche | Staying strictly in lane | Occasional relatable tangent (relationships, life) |
